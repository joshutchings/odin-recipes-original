Cold Brew

Cold brew is simply coffee that has been brewed with cold rather than hot water and usually involves a long steeping processâ€”anywhere between 12-24 hours. In terms of flavor, cold brew is generally characterized as smooth, low-acid, and heavier than its hot brewed counterparts.

Ingredients

  • Ground Coffee
  • Filtered Water

Steps

  1. Pour coffee grounds and cool water into a pitcher with a 1:8 (grounds:water) ratio.
  2. Refrigerate for 24 hours.
  3. Strain out coffee grounds three times through a clean towel.
  4. Pour into clean pitcher and serve with milk or cream.
